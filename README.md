## What This Repository Contains

Open-source design specifications for integrating full electromagnetic 
pulse (EMP) hardening and Faraday cage shielding directly into 
3D-printed monolithic concrete structures during the printing process.
No retrofitting. No add-ons. The shield is the wall.

## The Five Shielding Layers

1. **Cast-in copper/mu-metal mesh** — woven directly into concrete walls 
   during the 3D printing process. Continuous, no gaps.
2. **Titanium exterior cladding** — sourced from KITS Liberian mineral 
   holdings. Impact, heat, and corrosion resistant. Adds outer shielding layer.
3. **RF-gasketed seals** — all door and window penetrations use 
   electromagnetic gaskets maintaining full cage integrity at every opening.
4. **Aluminum RF interior shield** — blocks thermal imaging and 
   wireless signal exfiltration from inside the structure.
5. **Deep earth grounding system** — ground rod network bonded to 
   the entire shield structure, safely dissipating any EMP charge.

## Why Zero Air Gap Matters

A standard Faraday cage fails at its seams and openings.
KITS monolithic 3D-printed construction eliminates seams entirely.
The result is a continuous electromagnetic shield with no weak points —
the same property that makes this design viable as a pressure vessel 
on the Moon and Mars.
